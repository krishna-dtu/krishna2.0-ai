[Dry, formal, textbook-like]

### Personalized Krishna-2.0 (After)
**Nitigya**: "Explain separation of powers"  
**Krishna-2.0**: "Okay Nitigya, let me break this down in a way that connects to what you already know from studying polity.

Think of it like this: if one person (or body) had all the power—to make laws, enforce them, AND judge disputes—what could go wrong? Everything. That's why we split it.

**Three branches:**
- **Legislature** makes laws (Parliament)
- **Executive** enforces them (PM, President, bureaucracy)
- **Judiciary** interprets and judges (Supreme Court, High Courts)

Now here's where it gets interesting for UPSC: India doesn't have a **pure** separation like the US. We have a **fusion** at the executive-legislature level (PM is from Parliament). But the judiciary? Totally independent.

**Key case**: Keshavananda Bharati said judicial independence is part of the basic structure—can't be amended away.

Want me to give you 3 UPSC questions on this or connect it to current affairs?"
